üí≥ Credit Contracts and Consumer Finance Act (CCCFA)

What is the CCCFA?

The CCCFA regulates consumer credit to ensure responsible lending and protect borrowers from unaffordable debt. It applies to all consumer credit, including personal loans, credit cards, and buy-now-pay-later services.

Key Principles

Responsible Lending: We must only lend to customers who can afford repayments without suffering substantial hardship.

Affordability Assessment: Every loan requires thorough assessment of the borrower's financial situation.

Our CCCFA Obligations

1. Pre-Contractual Disclosure

Before entering any credit contract, customers must receive:

  • Initial Disclosure Statement: Key terms, costs, and customer rights
  • Total cost of credit including all fees and charges
  • Annual interest rate and how it's calculated
  • Default fees and when they apply
  • Right to cancel within specified timeframes
  • Credit repossession rights (if applicable)

2. Affordability Inquiries (Detailed Requirements)

Income Verification Must Include:

  • Employment verification: Payslips, employment contracts, IRD summaries
  • Self-employed income: Tax returns, bank statements, accountant statements
  • Benefit income: WINZ statements, income verification letters
  • Other income: Investment returns, rental income, child support
  • Income stability: Length of employment, likelihood of continuation

Expense Assessment Requirements:

  • Housing costs: Rent/mortgage, rates, insurance, maintenance
  • Utilities: Power, gas, water, telecommunications, internet
  • Food and groceries: Reasonable estimates based on household size
  • Transport: Vehicle costs, fuel, public transport, WOF, registration
  • Insurance: Life, health, income protection, vehicle
  • Dependents: Childcare, school costs, clothing, medical
  • Debt servicing: All existing credit commitments and minimum payments

3. Comprehensive Affordability Assessment Process

The assessment must determine if the customer can make payments without substantial hardship:

Assessment Methodology

  1. Net Income Calculation: Total verified income minus tax and other deductions
  2. Essential Expenses: Calculate minimum living costs using verified expenses or industry benchmarks
  3. Existing Debt Service: Include all current credit commitments
  4. Surplus Income: Net income minus essential expenses and existing debt
  5. Affordability Buffer: Maintain reasonable surplus for unexpected expenses
  6. Stress Testing: Consider potential changes in income or expenses

Documentation Requirements

Every affordability assessment must be documented with:

  • Evidence of income verification
  • Expense calculations and assumptions
  • Credit history and existing commitments
  • Assessment methodology and conclusions
  • Decision rationale (approve/decline)
  • Date, time, and assessor identification

4. Ongoing Responsibilities

  • Regular Statement Provision: Monthly or as agreed
  • Change in Circumstances: Monitor and respond to customer situations
  • Hardship Applications: Process within 10 working days
  • Variation Requests: Re-assess affordability for any changes
  • Default Management: Follow prescribed collection procedures

‚ö†Ô∏è Prohibited Practices - You Cannot:

  • Encourage overborrowing: Suggest higher amounts than requested or needed
  • Rush decisions: Pressure customers to sign immediately
  • Ignore red flags: Override obvious affordability concerns
  • Inadequate assessment: Skip or minimise verification steps
  • Misleading advertising: False or incomplete cost information
  • Unfair contract terms: Include unreasonable or oppressive conditions
  • Ignore hardship: Fail to consider or respond to customer difficulties

Specific Vulnerable Customer Protections

Enhanced care and consideration required for:

Financial Vulnerability Indicators:

  • Multiple credit applications in short timeframe
  • Existing high debt-to-income ratios
  • Previous defaults or financial hardship
  • Irregular or declining income patterns
  • High expense-to-income ratios

Personal Vulnerability Indicators:

  • Age-related: Very young (18-25) or elderly (70+) customers
  • Language barriers: Limited English proficiency
  • Cognitive impairment: Mental health issues, intellectual disability
  • Family violence: Coercion indicators, joint applications with concerns
  • Social isolation: Limited support networks

Enhanced Assessment for Vulnerable Customers

  • Additional verification and documentation
  • Extended cooling-off periods
  • Clear explanation of obligations and risks
  • Independent advice recommendations
  • Regular check-ins and monitoring
  • Escalation to senior staff for approval

CCCFA Penalties and Enforcement

Potential Consequences

  • Individual penalties: Up to $10,000 per breach
  • Company penalties: Up to $600,000 per breach
  • Licensing suspension: Loss of right to provide credit
  • Compensation orders: Repayment to affected customers
  • Reputation damage: Public enforcement action notices

üõ°Ô∏è Anti-Money Laundering and Countering Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT)

Overview of AML/CFT Act 2009

The AML/CFT Act requires financial institutions to detect and deter money laundering and terrorism financing. As a financial service provider, MatcheMe Money is a "reporting entity" with specific legal obligations.

Key Definitions

Money Laundering: The process of making illegally-gained proceeds appear legal by passing them through complex transactions.

Terrorism Financing: Providing funds or financial services to support terrorist activities.

Beneficial Owner: The natural person who ultimately owns or controls a customer.

Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Requirements

Standard Customer Due Diligence

For all customers, we must:

  • Verify Identity: Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver licence)
  • Verify Address: Utility bill, bank statement, rates notice (within 3 months)
  • Verify Date of Birth: Through primary identity document
  • Understand Nature and Purpose: Why they need our services
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Watch for unusual transactions or changes

Enhanced Customer Due Diligence (ECDD)

ECDD Required For:

  • Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs): Senior political figures and their associates
  • High-risk countries: Customers from FATF-identified high-risk jurisdictions
  • Complex ownership: Trusts, companies with unclear beneficial ownership
  • Unusual transactions: Large, complex, or suspicious activity patterns
  • Correspondent banking: Relationships with foreign financial institutions

ECDD Additional Requirements:

  • Senior management approval for establishing relationship
  • Enhanced ongoing monitoring of transactions
  • Additional information on source of funds/wealth
  • More frequent review of customer information
  • Detailed documentation of enhanced measures taken

Simplified Customer Due Diligence (SCDD)

Reduced CDD may apply for lower-risk customers such as:

  • NZ government departments and local authorities
  • NZ publicly listed companies
  • Other banks and financial institutions under AML supervision
  • Low-value transactions under specific thresholds

Important: SCDD Still Requires

  • Basic identity verification
  • Screening against sanctions lists
  • Ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Full CDD if risk profile changes

Ongoing Customer Due Diligence

Our responsibilities don't end after initial verification:

  • Transaction Monitoring: Review for unusual patterns or amounts
  • Regular Updates: Refresh customer information periodically
  • Beneficial Ownership: Monitor changes in corporate structure
  • Sanctions Screening: Regular checks against updated lists
  • Risk Assessment: Reassess customer risk levels over time

Suspicious Transaction Reporting

‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags - Report Immediately

  • Unusual transaction patterns: Sudden large deposits, round numbers
  • Inconsistent information: Customer details don't match documentation
  • Reluctance to provide information: Evasive about source of funds
  • Complex transactions: Unnecessarily complicated payment structures
  • Geographic concerns: Transactions involving high-risk countries
  • Cash-intensive businesses: Customers with predominantly cash operations
  • Rapid movement of funds: Money moving quickly through accounts

Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) Process

  1. Immediate escalation: Report to AML Compliance Officer within 24 hours
  2. Continue normal service: Don't alert customer to suspicions
  3. Document everything: Record all relevant information and observations
  4. Submit to FIU: File SAR with Financial Intelligence Unit within 3 working days
  5. Ongoing monitoring: Enhanced surveillance of customer activity

Sanctions and Watchlist Screening

Required Screening Lists

  • UN Security Council: Consolidated sanctions list
  • NZ Autonomous Sanctions: New Zealand specific sanctions
  • Designated Individuals: Terrorism suppression designations
  • PEPs Lists: Politically exposed persons databases

Screening Requirements:

  • Screen all new customers before establishing relationships
  • Screen beneficiaries of transactions
  • Regular re-screening of existing customers
  • Immediate screening when lists are updated

Record Keeping Requirements

Must Retain for 5 Years:

  • All customer due diligence documentation
  • Transaction records and supporting documents
  • Suspicious activity reports and analysis
  • Risk assessments and reviews
  • Training records and competency assessments
  • AML/CFT programme documentation

Wire Transfer Rules

International money transfers require additional information:

  • Originator information: Full name, address, account number
  • Beneficiary details: Name, address, account number
  • Purpose of transfer: Reason for sending money
  • Enhanced screening: Both sender and receiver screening

AML/CFT Penalties

  • Civil penalties: Up to $2 million for companies
  • Criminal penalties: Up to $600,000 and 2 years imprisonment
  • Licensing action: Registration cancellation or suspension
  • Directors liability: Personal responsibility for company breaches
  • Reputation damage: Public enforcement action

Your AML/CFT Responsibilities

Daily Compliance Actions

  • Verify customer identity before providing services
  • Screen customers against sanctions lists
  • Monitor transactions for suspicious patterns
  • Report unusual activity immediately
  • Maintain accurate and complete records
  • Complete regular AML/CFT training
  • Stay alert to new typologies and risks

üîí Privacy Act 2020

Overview

The Privacy Act 2020 protects individuals' personal information and gives them rights over how their information is collected, used, and disclosed.

Key Definition

Personal Information: Any information about an identifiable individual, including names, addresses, financial information, and even opinions about someone.

Your Daily Privacy Responsibilities

Every Day You Must:

  • Only collect personal information that's necessary
  • Explain to customers why you're collecting their information
  • Keep personal information secure and confidential
  • Only use information for authorised purposes
  • Never disclose information without proper authority
  • Respond promptly to access and correction requests
  • Report suspected breaches immediately
  • Stay current with privacy training and updates

The 13 Information Privacy Principles

Collection Principles (1-4)

Principle 1 - Purpose of Collection:

  • Only collect personal information for lawful purposes
  • Collection must be necessary for our business functions
  • Must be connected with our activities

Principle 2 - Source of Personal Information:

  • Collect directly from the individual where practicable
  • If collecting from third parties, ensure it's authorised
  • Consider privacy implications of indirect collection

Principle 3 - Collection of Information from Individual:

  • Inform customers when collecting their information
  • Explain the purpose of collection
  • Tell them who will receive the information
  • Advise of their right to access and correct information
  • Explain consequences of not providing information

Principle 4 - Manner of Collection:

  • Collect information fairly and lawfully
  • Don't collect by unlawful, unfair, or intrusive means
  • Don't collect excessively

Storage and Security (Principle 5)

Security Safeguards Required:

We must protect personal information with appropriate safeguards against:

  • Loss or unauthorised access
  • Use, modification, or disclosure
  • Other misuse
  • Damage or destruction

Access and Correction (Principles 6-7)

Principle 6 - Access to Personal Information:

  • Individuals can request access to their personal information
  • Must respond within 20 working days
  • Can charge reasonable fees (must advise in advance)
  • Some information may be withheld (legal privilege, etc.)
  • Must provide information in an intelligible form

Principle 7 - Correction of Personal Information:

  • Individuals can request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Must correct information if satisfied it's wrong
  • If correction refused, must attach individual's statement
  • Must take reasonable steps to notify others who received incorrect information

Accuracy (Principle 8)

We must ensure personal information is:

  • Accurate and up-to-date
  • Complete and relevant
  • Not misleading
  • Checked before use for important decisions

Retention (Principle 9)

Personal information should not be kept longer than necessary for the purposes we collected it. We must:

  • Have clear retention policies
  • Regularly review and delete unnecessary information
  • Consider legal requirements for retention periods
  • Securely dispose of information when no longer needed

Use and Disclosure (Principles 10-11)

‚ö†Ô∏è Principle 10 - Use of Personal Information

Limited Use Rule: Personal information can only be used for:

  • The purpose it was collected for
  • Directly related purposes the individual would reasonably expect
  • With the individual's consent
  • When required or permitted by law

‚ö†Ô∏è Principle 11 - Disclosure of Personal Information

Disclosure Restrictions: Can only disclose personal information:

  • For the purpose it was collected
  • With individual's consent
  • When required by law (court orders, legislation)
  • To prevent or lessen serious threat to public safety
  • For law enforcement purposes (with proper authority)

Disclosure Outside NZ (Principle 12)

Overseas Disclosure Requirements:

Before disclosing personal information outside NZ, ensure:

  • Individual has been informed of overseas disclosure
  • Individual has consented, or disclosure is otherwise permitted
  • Receiving country has comparable privacy protections
  • Consider using contractual safeguards

Unique Identifiers (Principle 13)

Restrictions on assigning and using unique identifiers:

  • Only assign unique identifiers when necessary
  • Don't adopt identifiers assigned by other organisations
  • Don't require individuals to disclose unique identifiers unless authorised
  • Keep unique identifiers secure and confidential

Privacy Breach Management

Immediate Response Steps:

If you suspect a privacy breach:

  1. Contain: Stop the breach from continuing or getting worse
  2. Assess: Determine what information was involved and who was affected
  3. Report: Notify your supervisor immediately
  4. Document: Record what happened, when, and what actions were taken
  5. Investigate: Find out how the breach occurred
  6. Notify: Inform affected individuals if required

Notifiable Privacy Breaches

Must Report to Privacy Commissioner within 72 hours if breach:

  • Causes serious harm: Or is likely to cause serious harm
  • Involves sensitive information: Health, financial, or other sensitive data
  • Affects many people: Large number of individuals impacted
  • Could enable identity theft: Or other serious harm
  • Involves systemic issues: Problems with security or processes

What constitutes "Serious Harm"?

  • Significant humiliation, hurt feelings, or loss of dignity
  • Damage to reputation or relationships
  • Financial loss or identity theft
  • Loss of business or employment opportunities
  • Physical or psychological harm
  • Discrimination or other unfair treatment

Individual Rights Under Privacy Act

Customers have the right to:

  • Know: What personal information we hold about them
  • Access: Request copies of their personal information
  • Correct: Have inaccurate information corrected
  • Complain: Lodge complaints about privacy breaches
  • Compensation: Seek damages for privacy violations

Privacy Act Penalties

  • Offence penalties: Up to $10,000 for individuals and agencies
  • Failure to notify breach: Up to $10,000 for not reporting notifiable breaches
  • Compensation orders: Payment to affected individuals through Human Rights Review Tribunal
  • Declaration orders: Public acknowledgment of breach
  • Reputation damage: Loss of customer trust
  • Civil action: Individuals can sue for damages

Daily Compliance Actions

Customer Interactions

Always:

  • Verify customer identity before discussing accounts
  • Explain why you need personal information
  • Keep conversations confidential
  • Document interactions accurately
  • Follow up on customer concerns promptly

Information Handling

‚ö†Ô∏è Security Checklist

  • Lock your computer when away
  • Don't discuss customer information in public
  • Shred confidential documents
  • Use secure email for sensitive information
  • Report lost devices immediately

Common Scenarios

Scenario 1: Customer Requests Credit Increase

CCCFA Response:

  • Treat as new credit application - full affordability assessment required
  • Consider cumulative effect of all existing and proposed credit
  • Verify any changes in income or circumstances since last assessment
  • Don't encourage unnecessary borrowing or suggest higher amounts
  • Document assessment thoroughly with updated evidence
  • Consider impact on customer's financial resilience

Scenario 2: Suspicious Transaction Detected

AML/CFT Response:

  • Don't alert the customer to your suspicions
  • Document all observations immediately
  • Report to AML Compliance Officer within 24 hours
  • Continue providing normal service levels
  • Gather additional information discreetly if possible
  • Monitor account for further unusual activity

Scenario 3: Customer Information Request

Privacy Act Response:

  • Verify customer identity using standard procedures
  • Provide information within 20 working days
  • Include all personal information held, not just account data
  • Explain any codes, abbreviations, or technical terms
  • Charge reasonable fees if applicable (must advise in advance)
  • Consider if any information should be withheld (legal privilege, etc.)

Scenario 4: Third Party Inquiry About Customer

Privacy Protection Response:

  • Don't confirm customer has accounts or relationships with us
  • Require written consent from customer for any information sharing
  • Check if legal exception applies (court order, law enforcement)
  • Escalate to supervisor if uncertain about legal requirements
  • Document the request and your response

Scenario 5: Customer Unable to Verify Identity

AML/CFT Response:

  • Cannot establish business relationship without proper CDD
  • Explore alternative verification methods if available
  • Consider if customer qualifies for simplified CDD
  • Provide clear explanation of requirements to customer
  • Document attempts and reasons for declining service
  • Consider if situation appears suspicious

Scenario 6: Vulnerable Customer Seeking Large Loan

CCCFA Enhanced Assessment:

  • Apply enhanced affordability assessment procedures
  • Consider recommending independent financial advice
  • Extend cooling-off period beyond minimum requirements
  • Ensure customer fully understands obligations and risks
  • Obtain senior management approval for decision
  • Implement enhanced ongoing monitoring if approved

When in Doubt - Escalation Process

Immediate Actions:

  1. Pause the interaction - don't make hasty decisions
  2. Consult your supervisor or relevant compliance team
  3. Document the issue and your concerns clearly
  4. Follow up with the customer within agreed timeframes
  5. Learn from the experience for future situations
